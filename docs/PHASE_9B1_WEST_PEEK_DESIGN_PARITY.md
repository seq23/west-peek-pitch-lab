# Phase 9B.1 — West Peek Design Parity Pass

## Status

Implemented as a Pitch Lab-only design parity correction after Phase 9B.

## Source-of-truth inspiration

The owner clarified that West Peek apps do not all have identical parity, and approved using these two as the preferred direction:

- `westpeek.ventures`
- `westpeek.live`

## Design findings used

### West Peek Ventures direction

Observed product posture:

- editorial venture language
- large simple founder-facing headline: “We back founders where can't be faked.”
- relationship/community thesis: community creates competitive advantage
- sharp founder/investor positioning
- simple navigation and direct CTA behavior
- trust-first, people-first, not generic SaaS chrome

### West Peek Live direction

Observed product posture:

- compact operating system language
- direct command center / product utility feel
- clear CTAs
- no fluffy interface copy
- event/production infrastructure credibility

## Corrected Pitch Lab design standard

Pitch Lab should now feel like the same family:

- black / white foundation
- restrained orange accent
- editorial, founder-facing language
- sharper card surfaces
- less rounded “generic AI SaaS” styling
- clear CTA hierarchy
- provided West Peek logo, not a fabricated placeholder mark
- relationship-first trust boundary

## Canonical brand assets

The provided West Peek logo image is now the canonical asset for this app:

- `public/assets/brand/west-peek-logo.jpg`
- `public/assets/brand/west-peek-logo.png`
- `public/assets/brand/west-peek-mark.png`

The earlier generated SVG logo/mark were removed because they were not the owner-provided logo.

## Locked mantra hierarchy

Main West Peek mantra:

> Good people should meet good people.

Pitch Lab product mantra:

> Good products need good stories.

## UX boundaries preserved

The design pass must not weaken these product truths:

- practice privately first
- share only when founder chooses
- explicit consent required
- submitted card creates pending intake only after confirmed handoff
- does not guarantee funding
- does not create automatic contact
- does not imply Scooter personally reviewed the pitch
- no email-me-card feature in MVP
- no public avatar render controls
- no fundability score

## Hostile review verdict

Phase 9B used the right colors but not enough actual app-family parity. Phase 9B.1 corrects that by:

1. replacing fabricated logo SVG assets with the provided West Peek mark;
2. reducing cream/gradient/rounded SaaS styling;
3. moving toward editorial black-white-orange surfaces;
4. borrowing the Ventures relationship/community posture and Live operating-system directness;
5. preserving all legal/trust/consent boundaries.

## Not proven

This pass does not prove pixel-perfect parity with live sites because the West Peek app family itself is not fully standardized. It does prove that Pitch Lab now follows the approved reference direction rather than a generic AI tool style.
